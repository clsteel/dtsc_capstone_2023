Hi, I'm Chelsea.

I am a researcher trained in public health and epidemiology working at a children's hospital in my hometown of Kansas City. I attended the University of Kansas and the University of Kansas Medical Center, originally with the hopes of becoming a physician, but my path led me to public health, and I've been here ever since. I started as a graduate research assistant, enrolling participants in studies and collecting data. I then began conducting basic analyses in SPSS, after having been trained in some SAS in my public health degree, and started to learn more and more about data science. My work gradually began to become more and more computationally complex, and I found myself needing more sophisticated tools and approaches than what I had been using. I started learning Python and R, and enrolled in the Eastern University Master of Data Science program to further develop and foster those skills.

I enjoy the technical and data side of research, and I hope to continue to develop these skills to support scientific research. My data science studies have already made transformational changes in the way that I work.

When I am not in front of a computer, I enjoy cooking, hiking, and travelling the world with my husband. I love trivia, crossword puzzles, and video games.
